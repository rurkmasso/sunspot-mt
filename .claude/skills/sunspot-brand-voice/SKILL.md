# Sunspot voice

Sunspot sounds like a Maltese local who actually goes to the beach.
Specific, slightly opinionated, never breathless.

## Do

- **Be specific.** "Sunbed 14, west-facing" beats "premium loungers".
  Bus numbers, opening times, road names, real prices.
- **Use Maltese place names without italics** — Għajn Tuffieħa,
  Mġarr ix-Xini, Ċirkewwa, Baħar iċ-Ċagħaq. Keep the diacritics;
  copy-paste them when typing is awkward.
- **Trust readers** with a 3-sentence paragraph and one piece of
  information they probably didn't know.
- **Lead with the useful detail** — price, time, distance, capacity —
  before the adjective.
- **Mention things you'd actually warn a friend about.** "Park
  at the church and walk down ~5 minutes." "By 11am the bay is
  jammed." "The mauve stingers turn up in late August."
- **Use Maltese context terms** when natural — Majjistral (NW wind),
  Grigal (NE), Xlokk (SE); luzzu boats; pastizzi; ftira; gbejna.
- **Stick to euros**, no $ or £ in body copy. Format as `€25`.

## Don't

- **"Stunning"**, **"breathtaking"**, **"luxury"**, **"experience"**
  as a noun, **"hidden gem"**, **"oasis"**, **"paradise"**,
  **"world-class"**, **"unforgettable"**, **"magical"**. Strike on
  sight.
- **Mediterranean stock-photography prose** — no "crystal-clear",
  "azure", "pristine", "turquoise jewel-toned" repeated more than
  once.
- **Treat Gozo as an afterthought.** It's half the offer.
- **Use emojis** as bullet points or decorative spacers. SVG icons
  only.
- **Translate Maltese words** you can leave in Maltese (lido, ftira,
  luzzu, salt pans).
- **Round numbers up.** "Roughly 200" not "around 200-ish".
- **Hedge with "perhaps"**. Either it's worth the trip or it isn't.

## Length

- **Summary** — 1 sentence, 12–20 words, fits in a card.
  Optimised for a tired thumb on a phone.
- **Description** — 2–4 sentences, 60–120 words. Reads in 20 seconds.
  Builds context the summary can't fit.
- **Long-form (guides, charter detail)** — pace yourself across
  3-paragraph sections with one editorial H2 each.

## Voice in the wild — examples

**Good:**

> Locals' favourite sandy bay west of Mġarr, between Lippija Tower and
> Fomm ir-Riħ. Walk-on, kiosk-only.

> Il-Gnejna is a wide sandy bay tucked under Lippija Tower on the
> north-west coast — a separate, quieter bay from Għajn Tuffieħa and
> Golden Bay just to the east. Loved by Maltese families. There's no
> commercial beach club — you bring your own towel, and a seasonal snack
> kiosk serves drinks and pastizzi.

**Bad (don't ship this):**

> Stunning hidden gem nestled along the breathtaking north-west coast
> of Malta. This pristine paradise offers crystal-clear waters and an
> unforgettable Mediterranean experience for the whole family.

## Headlines

H1s use Fraunces serif with `font-variation-settings: 'opsz' 60-72`.
Mix italic-amber accents for personality without exclamation marks:

> 112 ways to be *in the sea* today.
> Your own boat. *For the day.*
> Wet, fast, salty, *cold-shower-after.*

Avoid sentence-case banners. Don't use questions as H1s unless the
question is genuinely the page's job ("Visiting Malta?").

## Specific Sunspot rules

- The **"8% service fee"** is mentioned on every page where price
  appears. State it as a fact, not a virtue.
- "**Free cancellation up to 24 hours before**" is the standard
  policy and should be quoted verbatim where it appears.
- Sunspot's competitors are **walk-in chaos** and **a row of
  identical OTAs**. Position by being specific where they're vague.
- "**Built in Valletta**" appears in the footer everywhere. Don't
  repeat it inside body copy.
