Brand

The Sunspot identity is a small, deliberate kit: a sun mark with a single reflection glint, a serif wordmark set in Fraunces, and a palette pulled from late-afternoon sun on Sliema limestone. Use these assets for press, partner integrations, and operator decks.

The mark

A sun catching reflected water — the asymmetric glint at the 2 o'clock position is the bit you can't remove without losing the brand. Eight rays, three concentric circles, one offset highlight. Keep the proportions intact; never re-colour the glint.

Voice and tone

Sunspot sounds like a local who actually goes to the beach. Specific, slightly opinionated, never breathless. The aim is to be the trusted friend who knows which side of Ghajn Tuffieha gets shade after three — not the marketing copy that sells you a "Mediterranean experience."

Do

  • Be specific — "Sunbed 14, west-facing" beats "premium loungers"
  • Use Maltese place names without italicising them
  • Trust readers to handle a 3-sentence paragraph
  • Lead with the useful detail (price, time, distance) before the adjective
  • Mention things you'd actually warn a friend about

Don't

  • "Stunning," "breathtaking," "luxury," "experience"
  • Mediterranean stock photography or rose-gold gradients
  • Treat Gozo as an afterthought — it's half the offer
  • Use emojis as bullet points or decorative spacers
  • Translate Maltese words you can leave in Maltese
