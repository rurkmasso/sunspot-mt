Public transport from the airport

Taxi from airport: €18–25 to Sliema/St Julian's, €30–35 to Mellieha. Use the official airport taxi desk for fixed fares.

Getting to Comino & Gozo

Comino (Blue Lagoon):Boats from Ċirkewwa(€10–15 return) or Sliema(€25 return, includes other stops). First boat 09:00. Get there early — by noon it's chaos.

Gozo:Car ferry from Ċirkewwa. Foot passenger: free. With car: €15.70 return (you only pay coming back). Crossing 25 min, runs every 45 min.

Pro tip:for the Blue Lagoon, leave by 09:30 latest. By 11am the parking at Ċirkewwa fills up and tour boats jam the bay.

Sun & sea by season

Malta is swimmable June–October. Sea hits 26°C in August, drops to 19°C by November.

  • May:warm air (25°C), brisk sea (20°C). Pool clubs only.
  • Jun–Sep:peak. Beaches sell out weekends.
  • Oct:still warm (23°C air, 24°C sea), no crowds.

UV warning:11am–3pm in July/August is brutal. SPF 50, shade, hydrate.

What to bring

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (boutique pharmacies sell it for €15+; bring your own)
  • Water shoes — Malta's pretty bays are rocky
  • Refillable water bottle (tap water is desalinated and safe but tastes off; refill stations everywhere)
  • Light cover-up (shoulders covered for churches, even St John's Co-Cathedral)
  • Cash for tiny seafood shacks (cards rare in Mġarr ix-Xini, Wied iż-Żurrieq)

Money & tipping

Malta uses the Euro (€). Cards work nearly everywhere — even at sunbed kiosks. ATMs at every village.

Tipping isn't expected; round up at restaurants. Sunbed staff: €1–2 if they helped with umbrellas.

Sunspot bookings are 8% above operator price — clearly shown at checkout. No hidden fees.

Language

Everyone speaks English(an official language). Maltese (a Semitic-Latin hybrid) is also official. Italian is widely understood — many TV channels are Italian.

Useful phrases:

  • Bonġu— good morning
  • Bonswa— good evening
  • Grazzi— thank you
  • Mela— yeah / OK / sure (filler word, you'll hear it 50× a day)

Local quirks worth knowing

  • Sunday traditions:some restaurants & shops close. Beach clubs are busiest.
  • Feast days (festas):June–September every village has one. Fireworks, brass bands, late nights. Check the local calendar.
  • Driving:on the left (UK side). Roundabouts everywhere. Parking in Sliema/Valletta is brutal — public transport often faster.
  • Siesta:shops in villages close 13:00–16:00. Big retail in Sliema stays open.
  • Mosquitoes:bring repellent for evenings, especially near sea.

Eat like a local

  • Pastizzi— flaky pastries with ricotta or peas. €0.50 each. Breakfast of champions.
  • Ftira— Maltese flatbread with tuna, olives, capers. A meal.
  • Lampuki— dorado fish, in season Aug–Nov. Try at Marsaxlokk Sunday market.
  • Aljotta— garlicky fish soup. Best at Mġarr ix-Xini's Rew Rew shack.
  • Kinnie— Malta's national soft drink, bitter-orange. Try it once.
