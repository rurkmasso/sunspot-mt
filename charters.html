Skippered private charters across the Maltese islands — sailing yachts, catamarans, RIBs, power yachts and the occasional cabin charter for a long weekend. Same 8% service fee as the rest of Sunspot. The price you see is the price.

What we charter, and what it costs

Eleven private boats in the catalogue. They sit between the day-cruise catamarans (€55 a head, eighty strangers) and a bareboat charter from a marina nobody at your hotel can pronounce. You get the boat, the skipper, the crew if the boat needs one, and a sensible itinerary you can tweak by the hour.

Half-days are perfect for a Comino loop. Full-days reach Gozo and back with two anchor stops. Long weekends sleep aboard. Power yachts cut the travel time in half and add a chef on board. RIB convoys carry a group of twenty-four faster than any catamaran could.

Charter questions, answered.

Can I bring my own food and drink?

Yes — every operator allows it. Bring an ice-box, your own bottles, whatever you want. The catered options exist if you'd rather not, but nobody marks up corkage and nobody minds. Some boats provide a fridge.

Where do most charters depart from?

Most sailing and power yachts leave from Msida Marina. RIBs and catamarans leave from Sliema Ferries or Cirkewwa, depending on the trip. Sicily day-trips run from Mġarr in Gozo. The exact pontoon is in the night-before email.

What if the weather is bad?

The skipper makes the call by 18:00 the night before. If it's an operator cancellation for weather, you get a full refund within 24 hours — no need to argue about it. If the weather is just overcast and you want to skip anyway, that falls under the standard 72-hour cancellation policy.

Do I need a sailing license?

Not for any charter on this page — every boat ships with a licensed skipper. If you do hold a Day Skipper or higher and want to sail bareboat, get in touch (hello@sunspot.mt) and we'll match you with the operators who do that.

Are kids welcome?

On every boat except the bachelor catamaran. Lifejackets in your size are aboard. The pedalo and the banana boat over on the watersports page are more child-friendly for under-fives.

What's included in the price?

The boat, the crew (skipper, and a steward / chef where the listing says so), fuel, mooring fees and Sunspot's 8% service fee. Anything else is opt-in and clearly priced — DJ, decoration, additional catering, photographer.

Can I tip the skipper?

Yes, and it's welcome. Cash directly to the crew is standard — €20–50 per guest for a half-day, more for a full-day, scale with how much they did. Not included in the platform fee.

I want something not on this page.

Email hello@sunspot.mt with what you want and how many people. We work with twelve more operators we haven't listed yet — small wooden luzzu, Riva-style classics, a wooden gulet that sleeps twelve. We'll match the right boat.

Not sure which boat?

Tell us how many people, what date, and we'll come back with the two or three boats that fit. No upselling.
